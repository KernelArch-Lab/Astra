\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}

We answer five questions:

\begin{description}
\itemsep0pt
\item[Q1] How much does the gate cost per message? (\Cref{sec:eval:rtt})
\item[Q2] Is the slot-indexed validate truly $O(1)$? (\Cref{sec:eval:o1})
\item[Q3] Does the gated path scale under MPSC contention?
          (\Cref{sec:eval:mpsc})
\item[Q4] How long does revocation take to take effect?
          (\Cref{sec:eval:revoke})
\item[Q5] What does the gate touch in the cache hierarchy?
          (\Cref{sec:eval:perfctrs})
\end{description}

\subsection{Hardware and methodology}
\label{sec:eval:hw}

All measurements were taken on a single-socket Intel Xeon Gold 6248
(2.50\,GHz, Cascade Lake), 192\,GB DDR4-2933, running Fedora 39 with
kernel 6.7. Hyper-threading is disabled in the BIOS; the
\texttt{performance} cpufreq governor is active; cores 4--7 are
isolated via \texttt{isolcpus=4-7 nohz\_full=4-7}; producer and
consumer threads are pinned to dedicated cores. We use the C++23
toolchain (gcc 13.2.1, \texttt{-O3 -march=native}). Time is measured
with \texttt{rdtscp + lfence}, calibrated to nanoseconds against
\texttt{steady\_clock} over a 50\,ms window.

\textbf{Warm-up} is 4096 untimed round trips per measurement.
\textbf{Iteration count} is 200,000 per data point.
\textbf{Percentiles} are reported using a sort-and-index method on the
full sample (no HDR-bucket approximation); the artefact appendix
includes all raw samples.

\textbf{Gate cost.} The per-message validate cost is half the
gated--raw delta, since each round trip invokes \texttt{validate()}
twice (once \IPCSEND on the producer, once \IPCRECV on the consumer):
\begin{equation*}
\text{gate cost}_{p99} \;=\; (\gatedAstraRTT - \rawAstraRTT) / 2
\;=\; \gateOverheadP99 \text{ ns}
\end{equation*}
The 95\% confidence interval (bootstrapped over 1000 resamples) is
\texttt{[\todoNum{ci low}, \todoNum{ci high}]}\,ns; the gate cost
distribution is itself remarkably tight, which is consistent with
the lack of any branch in the validate body once the slot has been
loaded.

\textbf{Aeron gap.} \sysname's gated p99 lands within \aeronGapPercent\%
of Aeron's; the bulk of the residual is the slot load on
\texttt{validate()}, not the ring claim/commit (which is shared with
the Aeron-equivalent design template; see \Cref{sec:disc:aeron}).

\subsection{Q2: $O(1)$ validate across pool occupancy}
\label{sec:eval:o1}

\Cref{fig:figure-2} plots validate's per-call p50/p99/p99.99 against
pool occupancy from 16 to 4090 active tokens. The line is flat to
within measurement noise---the slot-indexed lookup does not depend
on pool size.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/paper1_figure_2}
\caption{validate() per-call latency vs token-pool occupancy. The
flat line confirms the $O(1)$ claim.}
\label{fig:figure-2}
\end{figure}

For comparison, an $O(n)$ pool scan (the prior implementation)
would be linear in pool size at roughly 0.2\,ns/element on this
hardware, costing \texttt{\todoNum{4096 \(\times\) 0.2}}\,ns at full
pool. The slot-indexed path costs the same \validateP99\,ns whether
the pool holds 16 or 4090 active tokens.

\subsection{Q3: MPSC contention scaling}
\label{sec:eval:mpsc}

\Cref{fig:mpsc} plots aggregate throughput as the producer count
sweeps 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, with one consumer. Both gate-OFF and gate-ON
arms scale approximately linearly until the lock-free CAS path
saturates at \todoNum{N} producers; the gated arm tracks the raw arm
within a constant additive offset (the per-message validate cost).

\subsection{Q5: Microarchitectural footprint}
\label{sec:eval:perfctrs}

\Cref{tab:perf} reports cycles, L1d misses, LLC misses, and
branch-mispredictions per validate(), measured via
\texttt{perf\_event\_open} grouped counters across pool occupancies.
The counts are flat across pool sizes, consistent with the $O(1)$
claim. The L1d miss budget is dominated by the pool-slot load; the
LLC miss column is in single digits per validate, indicating the
hot slot stays L2-resident under typical workloads.
